Choose your Unit below

  • Unit 1

    Functions

    Grade 11 Unit 1 on functions introduces students to the concept of a function as a unique relationship between input and output values. Students explore function notation, identify domain and range, and analyze various types of functions, including linear, quadratic, and reciprocal, to understand their behavior and real-world applications.

  • Unit 2

    Rational Expressions

    Grade 11 Unit 2 on rational expressions teaches students to work with algebraic expressions that involve fractions with polynomial numerators and denominators. Students learn to simplify, multiply, divide, add, and subtract rational expressions, as well as solve equations involving rational expressions while identifying any restrictions on the variable.

  • Unit 3

    Transformations

    Grade 11 Unit 3 on transformations focuses on translating, reflecting, stretching, and compressing functions on the Cartesian plane. Students learn how these transformations affect the graphs of functions, such as linear, quadratic, and exponential functions, and use this understanding to model real-world scenarios.

  • Unit 4

    Exponential Functions

    Grade 11 Unit 4 on exponential functions introduces students to exponential growth and decay, exploring functions of the form \( y = a \cdot b^x \) where \( b > 0 \) and \( b \neq 1 \). Students learn to graph exponential functions, interpret key features such as the y-intercept and asymptote, and apply these concepts to model real-life situations like population growth and radioactive decay.

  • Unit 5

    Trigometric Geometry

    Grade 11 Unit 5 on trigonometric geometry builds on trigonometric ratios and extends them to solve problems involving non-right triangles. Students learn to apply the Law of Sines and the Law of Cosines to find unknown side lengths and angles in oblique triangles, as well as solve real-world problems involving navigation, architecture, and physics.

  • Unit 6

    Trigometric Functions

    Grade 11 Unit 6 on trigonometric functions introduces students to the concepts of sine, cosine, and tangent as periodic functions. Students explore the properties of these functions, including amplitude, period, and phase shift, and learn to graph and analyze trigonometric functions to model real-world phenomena such as sound waves and seasonal temperature changes.

  • Unit 7

    Discrete Functions

    Grade 11 Unit 7 on discrete functions focuses on functions where the variable takes on only specific, distinct values, often represented by sets of points or sequences. Students explore patterns in arithmetic and geometric sequences, learn to model discrete data, and apply these concepts to solve problems in areas like finance and computer science.
